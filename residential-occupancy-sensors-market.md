<p><strong>The Residential Occupancy Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Residential occupancy sensors are devices used in homes to detect the presence or absence of people in a specific area. PIR sensors work by detecting infrared radiation emitted by humans, making them ideal for detecting motion. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to identify movement and are effective in large spaces. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies to provide more accurate detection. Other types of sensors include microwave sensors and image-based sensors. These sensors are increasingly used in homes to improve energy efficiency and enhance security.</p></p>
