<p><p>Straight seam submerged arc welded pipe refers to a type of pipe that is produced by longitudinally welding a hot-rolled steel strip using a submerged arc welding process. This welding technique involves the formation of an electric arc between the workpiece and a continuously fed bare metal electrode. The arc, shielded by a layer of granulated flux, melts the workpiece edges, and they are then pressed together to form a weld seam.</p><p>The current outlook for the straight seam submerged arc welded pipe market is positive, and it is expected to witness significant growth in the forecasted period.
