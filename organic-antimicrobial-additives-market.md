<p><p>The organic antimicrobial additives market is divided into three types: OBPA, DCOIT, and Triclosan. OBPA (2-Butyl-1,2-benzisothiazolin-3-one) is widely used as a biocide in various products to prevent the growth of bacteria, fungi, and algae. DCOIT (4,5-dichloro-2-n-octyl-3(2H)-isothiazolone) is known for its effectiveness against mold, algae, and bacteria, making it an essential additive for industrial coatings, paints, and marine applications. Triclosan is commonly used in personal care products, such as soaps and toothpaste, for its antibacterial properties.</p></p>
