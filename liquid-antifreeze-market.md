<p><ul><li>Alcohols</li><li>Alcohol Ether</li><li>Chlorinated Hydrocarbons</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The liquid antifreeze market can be broadly categorized into different types: alcohols, alcohol ether, chlorinated hydrocarbons, and other markets. Alcohols, such as ethylene glycol and propylene glycol, are commonly used as antifreeze agents due to their low freezing point and high heat transfer capacity. Alcohol ether products, such as ethylene glycol monomethyl ether, exhibit excellent solvency and are used in various industrial applications. Chlorinated hydrocarbons, like dichloromethane and trichloroethylene, are known for their degreasing properties and are utilized as antifreeze solvents in specific applications. Lastly, the "other" market includes alternative antifreeze solutions that offer unique benefits or cater to niche industries.</p></p>
