<p><p>The automotive aluminum market consists of three types: cast aluminum, rolled aluminum, and extruded aluminum. Cast aluminum refers to aluminum components that are made by pouring molten aluminum into molds and allowing it to cool and solidify. Rolled aluminum is created by passing aluminum ingots through a series of rollers to reduce their thickness and improve their strength. Extruded aluminum involves forcing heated aluminum through a die to create complex shapes and profiles. These three types play significant roles in the production of various automotive parts and structures.</p></p>
