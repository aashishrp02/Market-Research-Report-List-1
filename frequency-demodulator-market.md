<p><p>Frequency demodulators are used to extract the original message signal from a frequency-modulated (FM) carrier wave. The market for FM demodulators includes various types like slope FM detector, coincidence FM demodulator, ratio detector, phase-locked loop FM demodulator, and quadrature FM demodulator. Slope FM detectors convert frequency changes into voltage variations. Coincidence FM demodulators perform demodulation by comparing the input signal with a reference clock signal. Ratio detectors measure the ratio of the frequency deviation to the FM carrier frequency. Phase-locked loop FM demodulators use feedback to lock onto the carrier signal, while quadrature FM demodulators separate the FM signal into two quadrature components for demodulation.</p></p>
