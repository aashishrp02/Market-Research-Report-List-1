<p><p>Carbon Fiber Reinforced Thermoplastic Composites (CFRTP) are advanced materials made by combining carbon fibers with a thermoplastic polymer matrix. There are two main market types for CFRTP: Polyacrylonitrile (PAN)-based CFRTP and Pitch-based CFRTP. PAN-based CFRTP is produced using polyacrylonitrile fibers, which are oxidized, carbonized, and then treated with resin to create the final material. Pitch-based CFRTP, on the other hand, is made by carbonizing petroleum-based pitch fibers. Both market types offer excellent strength-to-weight ratios and high resistance to heat, making them preferred materials for various industries, including aerospace and automotive.</p></p>
