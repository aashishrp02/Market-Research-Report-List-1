<p><p>Low iron silicate glass is a type of glass that has a reduced iron content, giving it improved clarity and color neutrality compared to regular glass. It finds applications in various industries. Rolled glass refers to a production process where molten glass is fed between rollers to create thin, even sheets. It is commonly used in greenhouse construction, solar panels, and automotive applications. Float glass, on the other hand, is produced by pouring molten glass onto a bed of molten tin, creating a smooth and uniform sheet. It is widely used in architectural windows, mirrors, and display screens.</p></p>
