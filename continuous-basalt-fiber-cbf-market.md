<p><p>Continuous Basalt Fiber (CBF) is a type of fiber derived from basalt rocks, known for its exceptional strength and durability. In the market, there are three types of CBF production methods. Centrifugal-blowing involves spinning molten basalt rocks at high speeds to create fine fibers. Centrifugal-multiroll method uses rotating rollers to generate fibers. Lastly, die-blowing passes molten basalt rocks through a small nozzle to form continuous fibers. These methods allow for the production of various CBF products for applications such as construction, automotive, and aerospace industries.</p></p>
