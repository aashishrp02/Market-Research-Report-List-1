<p><p>Electron microscopy is a technique used to study objects at a very high resolution by using a beam of electrons instead of light. There are several types of electron microscopy, including Transmission Electron Microscope (TEM), Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM), and others. TEM allows for the visualization of internal structures of samples, while SEM provides detailed surface imaging. Other types may include environmental SEM, low-voltage SEM, and high-resolution SEM. These different types cater to specific research needs and provide valuable insights into the fine details and composition of various materials and biological samples.</p></p>
