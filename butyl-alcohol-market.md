<p><p>Butyl alcohol is available in two main market types: bio-butyl alcohol and chemical butyl alcohol. Bio-butyl alcohol is produced through the fermentation of biomass, such as corn or sugarcane, and is considered a renewable alternative to petroleum-based chemicals. On the other hand, chemical butyl alcohol is derived from the chemical synthesis of fossil fuels. Both types have various industrial applications, including solvents, coatings, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals. Bio-butyl alcohol is gaining popularity due to its environmentally friendly and sustainable nature, while chemical butyl alcohol remains widely used in industries.</p></p>
