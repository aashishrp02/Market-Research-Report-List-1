<p><p>Structural glass curtain walls are non-load-bearing exterior wall systems that utilize glass panels to enclose buildings, allowing natural light to penetrate the space while providing thermal insulation and weather resistance. These systems are typically supported by aluminum frames or steel mullions and are widely used in modern architecture to create visually appealing and energy-efficient buildings.</p><p>The global structural glass curtain walls market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 6.20% during the forecast period. Several factors drive this market growth. Firstly, the increasing demand for sustainable building materials and energy-efficient designs fuels the adoption of structural glass curtain walls. Moreover, the expanding construction industry, particularly in emerging economies, plays a crucial role in the market's prosperity. Additionally, the rising popularity of green buildings and stringent government regulations promoting energy conservation further contribute to market growth.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is a growing preference for etched and fritted glass in structural glass curtain walls. These types of glass provide privacy while still allowing natural light transmission. Moreover, innovations such as the integration of photovoltaic cells into the glass panels are gaining traction, enabling buildings to generate renewable energy. The adoption of point-supported glass curtain wall systems, which offer a sleek and minimalist aesthetic, is also on the rise. Furthermore, advancements in glass technology, such as the production of larger and curved glass panels, are creating new design possibilities for architects and builders.</p><p>Overall, the structural glass curtain walls market is expected to experience steady growth due to the increasing demand for sustainable and visually appealing building solutions. The incorporation of advanced glass technologies and the rise of energy-efficient construction practices will continue to drive market expansion in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Structural Glass Curtain Walls Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Unitised Type</li><li>Stick Built Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The structural glass curtain walls market comprises two main types: unitised type and stick-built type. The unitised type involves pre-assembled glass panels that are manufactured off-site and then installed as complete units onto the building's structure. This type offers faster installation and a higher degree of weather tightness. On the other hand, stick-built type involves the on-site assembly of individual components, which allows for more flexibility in design and customization. Both types play a crucial role in creating aesthetically pleasing and energy-efficient facades for buildings.</p></p>
