<p><p>Enzymatic biodiesel refers to the production of biodiesel using enzymes instead of traditional chemical catalysts. Enzymes are proteins that act as catalysts and speed up chemical reactions. In the case of biodiesel production, enzymes are used to convert vegetable oils or animal fats into biodiesel through a process called transesterification. Enzymatic biodiesel production offers several advantages over traditional methods, such as lower energy consumption and milder reaction conditions, resulting in higher yield and better quality biodiesel.</p><p>The future outlook for the enzymatic biodiesel market is promising.
