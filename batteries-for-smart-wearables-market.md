<p><p>The batteries used in smart wearables fall into two main types: Li-Ion (Lithium-Ion) and Li-Po (Lithium-Polymer). Li-Ion batteries have become widely adopted due to their higher energy density, longer lifespan, and lower self-discharge rate. They are commonly found in smartphones and laptops. Conversely, Li-Po batteries are lighter, more flexible, and have a lower profile, making them suitable for thin and compact devices like smartwatches and fitness trackers. Both types offer a reliable power source for smart wearables, with Li-Ion being more prevalent in larger devices and Li-Po in smaller, slimmer devices.</p></p>
