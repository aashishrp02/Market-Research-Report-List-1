<p><p>Cross-linked polyethylene insulated cables are widely used in various industries due to their excellent properties such as high temperature resistance, durability, and flexibility. These cables are available in different market types, including 1 core and 3 core. </p><p>A 1 core cable consists of a single conductor surrounded by a cross-linked polyethylene insulation layer. It is commonly used in applications where a single power or signal transmission is required, such as in household wiring or industrial control systems.</p><p>On the other hand, a 3 core cable comprises three conductors, each insulated and surrounded by a cross-linked polyethylene layer. This type of cable is commonly used for applications where three-phase power transmission is necessary, such as in power distribution networks or large-scale industrial installations.</p><p>Overall, the choice between 1 core and 3 core cables depends on the specific requirements of the application, such as the type of power transmission and the number of phases needed.</p></p>
